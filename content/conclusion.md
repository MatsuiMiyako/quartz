## Final Answer to My Inquiry Question

> *"How does the electrolyte concentration of different sports drinks affect their electrical conductivity, and what does this reveal about their effectiveness as electrolyte replacements?"*

Going into this experiment I assumed that Gatorade would come out on top because it's the most marketed sports drink and the one athletes reach for most (it's my favorite too!). But the results speak for themselves, and honestly that made this project way more interesting than I expected.

The electrolyte concentration of a drink directly determines how well it conducts electricity. Drinks with more dissolved ions, specifically Na⁺, K⁺, and Cl⁻, allowed more current to flow between the probes, producing a brighter LED and a higher brightness score. Coconut water led the results at 122/255, followed by my homemade solution (101/255), Gatorade (99/255), Powerade (95/255), and plain water at the bottom (29/255). This tells us that a drink's effectiveness as an electrolyte replacement isn't just about branding but it comes down to how many free ions it actually puts into solution. By that measure, coconut water is the most effective electrolyte replacement of the five drinks I tested, which I did not expect at all.
