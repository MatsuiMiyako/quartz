## Area of Interest: Health & Medicine

Specifically sports drinks and electrolytes.

---

## Connections to Chemistry

This project connects to **two Chemistry 30S units:**

### Unit 1: Solutions & Acids/Bases

- Electrolytes are ionic compounds that dissolve in water to form a solution
- The concentration of dissolved ions determines how conductive the solution is
- This connects directly to the concept of **solutes, solvents, and solution concentration**

### Unit 2: Properties of Matter

- Electrical conductivity is a measurable physical property of matter
- Different drinks have different physical properties based on their chemical makeup
- Comparing conductivity across drinks demonstrates how composition affects properties

## Brainstormed Possible Questions

1. _I wonder how the electrolyte concentration in different sports drinks (Gatorade, Powerade, coconut water) affects how well they conduct electricity, and what that tells us about their effectiveness in the body?_

2. _What if I compared a homemade electrolyte solution to commercial sports drinks — would it conduct electricity just as well at the same ion concentration?_

3. _I wonder how diluting a sports drink with water changes its conductivity, and at what point it becomes too weak to effectively replace electrolytes lost through sweat?_

---

## My Refined Question

> How does the electrolyte concentration of different sports drinks affect their electrical conductivity, and what does this reveal about their effectiveness as electrolyte replacements?

## Why I Chose This Topic

I chose this topic under **Health & Medicine** because sports drinks are something athletes use every day — but most people don't actually know what makes one drink better than another. I wanted to use chemistry to find a real answer.
