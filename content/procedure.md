## Part A: Building the Conductivity Tester

**Step 1:** Connect the battery holder and LED together in a simple series circuit using SNAP circuits pieces.

**Step 2:** Leave one end of the circuit open — this gap is where the liquid will complete the circuit.

**Step 3:** Attach a wire to each open end. Wrap a small square of aluminum foil around the tip of each wire to create electrodes (probes).

**Step 4:** Test the tester by dipping both probes into water (without letting them touch). The LED should light up dimly, confirming the circuit works.

> Note: It will light up very dimly in tap water because tap water is not 100% pure water; it has trace amounts of minerals in it as well.

## Part B: Running the Experiment

**Step 1:** Label 5 cups: Plain Water, Gatorade, Powerade, Coconut Water, Homemade Solution.

**Step 2:** Pour equal amounts (~50 mL) of each drink into its labeled cup.

**Step 3:** Start with plain water as the control. Dip both probes into the cup.

**Step 4:** Observe and record the LED brightness using your phone.

**Step 5:** Dry both probes thoroughly with a paper towel.

**Step 6:** Repeat for each drink.

**Step 7:** Calculate the average brightness for each drink.
