## Coconut water outperformed commercial drinks

Coconut water had the highest conductivity with a brightness score of 122/255, followed closely by Gatorade (99/255), homemade solution (101/255), and Powerade (95/255). Plain water had the lowest score at 29/255. This result tells us that coconut water has a higher total dissolved ion concentration than Gatorade or Powerade per serving.

---
## Why plain water still conducted electricity

Plain water (H₂O) is a molecular compound, not an ionic one. When water molecules dissolve, they do not break apart into ions, they remain as neutral H₂O molecules. Because there are no free charged particles in pure water, there is nothing to carry the electrical current between the two probes, so the LED does not light up. Due to tap water not being 100% pure water, its minerals conduct a little bit of electricity. 

---

## Homemade vs commercial

The homemade solution scored 101/255, placing it between Gatorade (99/255) and Powerade (95/255), meaning a simple kitchen recipe made from water, table salt, , and lemon juice matched and even slightly outperformed both commercial sports drinks in terms of electrical conductivity. This suggests that the Na⁺ and Cl⁻ ions provided by table salt, combined with the K⁺ from lemon juice, were sufficient to produce an ion concentration comparable to that of Gatorade and Powerade.

## Sources of error and improvements

| Source of Error                          | How It Could Affect Results            | How to Fix It                            |
| ---------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------- |
| Residue left on probes between tests     | Cross-contaminates samples             | Rinse with distilled water, not just dry |
| Room lighting affecting photo brightness | Makes LED photos hard to compare       | Always shoot in same darkened room       |
| Temperature differences between drinks   | Warmer liquids conduct slightly better | Test all drinks at same temperature      |
