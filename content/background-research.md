## What Are Electrolytes?

Electrolytes are minerals that dissolve in water to form ions, atoms or molecules that carry an electrical charge. The most important electrolytes in sports drinks include:

| Ion | Symbol | Role in the Body |
|---|---|---|
| Sodium | Na⁺ | Controls fluid balance, nerve signals |
| Potassium | K⁺ | Muscle contractions, heart function |
| Chloride | Cl⁻ | Balances fluids, stomach acid |
| Magnesium | Mg²⁺ | Muscle and nerve function |

When you sweat, your body loses these ions. If they aren't replaced, muscles cramp, energy drops, and performance suffers.

---

## Why Do Ions Conduct Electricity?

Pure water is actually a **poor conductor** of electricity. This is because water molecules (H₂O) are neutral, meaning they have no charge to carry current.

When an ionic compound like salt (NaCl) dissolves in water, it separates into ions:

$$NaCl \rightarrow Na^+ + Cl^-$$

These free-moving charged particles can carry electrical current through the solution. The more ions present, the better the solution conducts electricity.

> Therefore, higher ion concentration = higher conductivity = brighter LED in our experiment

---

## How Sports Drinks Work

Sports drinks are carefully formulated solutions designed to:
1. Replace water lost through sweat (rehydration)
2. Replace electrolytes (primarily sodium and potassium)
3. Provide energy through sugars (glucose/sucrose)

The electrolyte content is listed on the nutrition label in milligrams (mg). A higher mg value means more ions dissolved in the drink — which should show up as higher conductivity in our experiment.

## Chemistry Vocabulary

- **Ion:** An atom or molecule with a positive or negative electrical charge
- **Electrolyte:** A substance that produces ions when dissolved in water
- **Conductivity:** The ability of a solution to carry electrical current
- **Concentration:** The amount of solute dissolved in a given amount of solution
- **Solute:** The substance being dissolved (e.g. electrolyte minerals)
- **Solvent:** The liquid doing the dissolving (e.g. water)
